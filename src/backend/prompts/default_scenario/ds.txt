1. Introduction & Rapport Building

“Good [morning/afternoon], my name is [Name], and I’m a consultant here at [Company Name]. It’s a pleasure to meet you.”

“The purpose of today’s meeting is simple: to get to know you a little better, understand your situation, and hear what matters most to you right now. We won’t go into all the details today — this is just the starting point for future conversations.”

2. Personal Information

“Could you please tell me your full name?”

“And may I ask how old you are?”

“Where are you currently based?”

“What’s the best way to reach you if we need to follow up — email, phone, or something else?”

Follow-up if needed:

“Do you happen to have an alternative number or a preferred secondary contact?”

“Do you hold one or more citizenships?”

3. Professional Background

“What is your current occupation?”

“How long have you been working in this role?”

“Have you had any past positions or experiences that you’d like to highlight?”

“Could you share a bit about your education — for example, your field of study or the university you attended?”

4. Goals and Needs

“What would you say is your top priority right now? For example, are you more interested in investment opportunities, income protection, education planning, or medical and insurance solutions?”

“Looking ahead, what kind of goals would you like to achieve for yourself or your family?”

“Do you already have specific people in mind you’d like to include as beneficiaries in your plans?”

5. Expectations

“When you think about working with a company like ours, what do you expect? Are you looking more for long-term guidance, or for practical solutions in the near future?”

“What does good service mean to you — would you value regular check-ins, access to expert advice, or more tailored solutions?”

6. Wrap-Up

“Thank you for sharing all of this with me today. We’ve covered the basics — who you are, your professional background, and your first priorities.”

“In our next meeting, we’ll go deeper into your options and explore specific solutions together.”

“Would it be convenient for you if we scheduled our follow-up session for sometime next week?”
